Game of Gomoku


Gomoku, also called Five in a Row or Naughts and Crosses, is an strategy board game. It is traditionally played with Go pieces(black and white stones) on a Go board(goban), using 15x15 of the 19x19 grid intersections. The goal of the game is to place unbroken chain of five stones horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Rules

Standard - The most basic variation, black player starts the game, game ends when one player formed unbroken chain of five(not more) stones. This variations of the game has been solved, black player who plays perfect moves has so called "sure win".

Swap - Because "Standard" variation has been solved, players seek for a solution to make game more fair. In this variation black player starts with placing three stones on the board(2 black stones and one white stone), and then white player has to choose to play black or white stones.

Swap2 - Swap rule was a main variation for a few years, however it still wasn't perfect. In swap2 variation black player starts with placing three stones, then white player has three options, choose black stones, choose white stones or place additional two stones of the board, if additional stones are placed then black player is choosing the color. Swap2 variation is official one to play in tournaments.

History

Gomoku has existed in Japan since the Meiji Restoration. The name "Gomoku" is from the Japanese language, in which it is referred to as gomokunarabe (五目並べ). Go means five, moku is a counter word for pieces and narabe means line-up. The game is also popular in Korea, where it is called omok (오목 [五目]) which has the same structure and origin as the Japanese name. The Japanese call this game Go-moku (five stones). In the nineteenth century, the game was introduced to Britain where it was known as Go Bang, said to be a corruption of the Japanese word goban, said to be adopted from Chinese k'i pan (qí pán) "go-board."
